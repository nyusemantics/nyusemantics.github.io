Strengthening and scalemate association in the modal domain

Paloma Jeretič, NYU

Abstract

There is cross-linguistic and language-specific evidence that functional root modals like can and must are merged below sentential negation in the syntax. However, in many cases, root modals have a wide scope interpretation. Curiously, only necessity modals do (a generalization made from a significant set of languages from different families, cf. de Haan 1997, Iatridou & Zeijlstra 2013). Below are a few examples of these wides scope readings.

(1) a. You must not go. (translation for b-c-d)
      b. Il ne faut pas y aller. [French]
      c. Tu ne dois pas y aller. (ambiguous with "you don't have to go") [French]
      d. No debes ir. [Spanish]

I propose a novel analysis for these interpretations, giving arguments from the interaction of negation and French necessity modals falloir (1b) and devoir (1c).

Namely, I argue that the wide scope interpretation of falloir and devoir is not achieved by raising above negation (as argued in Homer 2011, Iatridou & Zeijlstra 2013), but by leaving the modal in situ, and strengthening the negated necessity expression to the equivalent of a wide scope reading of the modal.

I assimilate this analysis with other accounts of strengthening of connectives and quantifiers cross-linguistically (Bowler 2014, Bassi & Bar-Lev 2016, Singh et al 2016), that are the result of a grammaticalized implicature (à la Fox 2007) of an expression containing a lexical item that lacks a stronger lexicalized scalemate. I give independent arguments for falloir lacking a weaker scalemate (i.e. a possibility modal), which results in strengthening in the presence of negation. I also argue that devoir optionally associates with a scalemate.

Finally, I discuss how the strengthening analysis provides a hypothesis for the typological asymmetry between root necessity and possibility modals in their interaction with negation, as long as they have to stay in situ: the wide scope necessity reading is a result of strengthening the weak ¬□ expression, while the wide scope possibility reading is underivable by strengthening, since ¬◇ is already strong.
